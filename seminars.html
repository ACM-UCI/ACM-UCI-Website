Thursday, April 19th
DBH 4011		 Proving Statements without revealing proofs. The Power of Interaction in Proof Systems
Abstract:
What if you were able to prove a statement without ever revealing the proof. In a sense you just convinced somebody of the validity of a statement by having a conversation with them. They learn nothing more than that the statement is true or false. What if you could prove a statement by just randomly picking points and checking validity on those points. In this talk I introduce Interactive Proof systems and talk about Zero Knowledge and PCP. If none of this interests you here are a couple of riddles that may: Flip a coin over a telephone with a person you don’t trust. Can you construct a protocol that guarantees a fair result that both parties agree on? Or a protocol where you can play poker over the phone? If any of these questions stump you, then this talk will answer it for you

Abstract:

Thursday, May 24th
DBH 4011		 Twisting your Brain with Pure Functional Programming and Haskell
Abstract:
Functional programming is a programming paradigm that is growing in popularity, but how often do we deal with it in our classes? Sure, imperative languages like Java, Python, and even C++ have some built-in features to support functional programming, but at the end of the day, these languages still treat everything, even functions, as objects. What if we could learn to think purely in terms of functions? That’s exactly what purely functional programming languages like Haskell can help us do.
